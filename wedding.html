Location


Greene Family Camp is where Natalie spent most of her summers growing up. It's a peaceful place where we can all spend time together and celebrate. The camp has central air conditioning, including bunks. There are tennis and basketball courts, a game room with ping pong, arts and crafts, and even a lake!

We would love for all of our guests to stay the weekend at camp with us. The cost for the weekend includes lodging and all meals. Tent camping is free. Payment is included with RSVP.

Getting to Camp

Greene Family Camp is located in the heart of Texas, in the town of Bruceville. The city of Waco, TX is only fifteen minutes away. We recommend flying into Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW), since flights into Austin will be more expensive due to the SXSW Music Festival.

Accomodations

Camp has individual rooms in a block and bunks. Individual rooms have private bathrooms. Bunks have shared bathrooms.

Please note that this is a summer camp and that the rooms are very basic; most rooms have twin and bunk beds (no double beds). We will be placing everyone in housing before the wedding weekend. Camp will supply linens, pillows, and towels, but guests are welcome to bring their own bedding.

Meals

Meals will be available for the duration of the weekend. We will provide dinner on Friday, all meals on Saturday, and breakfast on Sunday. Please let us know about dietary restrictions when you RSVP.


Packing List


March in Texas can mean cold, hot, perfect, rainy, or any combination! We're keeping our fingers crossed for ideal weather, but please consider bringing the following:

During the day

  • Comfortable footwear
  • Umbrella in case of rain
  • A sweater or sweatshirt for the evening
  • Musical instruments
  • Tennis racket, frisbees, or other sporting supplies. There's a volleyball net, tennis courts, baseball fields, a soccer field, and basketball courts. All balls are supplied by camp.
  • Yoga clothing and mat: Ross's sister Elissa will be leading Yoga on Saturday and all are invited to join!
  • Waterbottle
  • Bugspray

If you're staying on camp

  • Toiletries
  • Flip flops for the shower
  • Optional: Extra linens. Camp provides its own linens and towels, but you are encouraged to bring your own if you want!
  • Flashlight: Camp has lamps throughout the grounds, but some trails can get dark at night
  • Tent: If you're camping, please bring your own tent/sleeping bag/towels. You'll be able to use bunk showers and bathrooms.

The Ceremony


The wedding ceremony and reception will take place outdoors, and guests will sit on benches. Please make yourself at ease by wearing comfortable clothing and flat shoes. Wedding attire is informal; a sundress or button-down shirt and slacks are fine.

The ceremony will be led by Rabbi Michael Mellen, will include Jewish customs and some Hebrew, and should last about 30 minutes. Prior to the ceremony, we will sign a Ketubah, or a Jewish marriage contract.
